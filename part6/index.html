Archiving on the Internet

What is the pupose of an artist archive?

Jessie McCarty, who led An Index is a Diary workshop at Index, discussed the purpose of a personal archive as a diary. To begin, we look to the definition of a diary in the context of understanding indexes and the artist's collection. First they bring attention to the definition of an index, a catalog, and a diary.

INDEX

noun

an alphabetical list of names, subjects, etc., with references to the places where they occur, typically found at the end of a book.

An other word for an index could be refered to as a record of names, subjects, etc.

CATALOG

noun

a complete list of items, typically one in alphabetical or other systematic order.

vs. Brtish definition, a datebook or filled with dates.

DIARY

noun

a book in which one keeps a daily record of events and experiences.

vs. Brtish definition, a datebook or filled with dates.

The purpose of an artist archive is to preserve, document, unravel, research, unveil, to look at [feeling] with eyes wide open, to carry, and finally, describe.

— Jessie McCarty

"Homer’s catalog is not just a list but a list of lists, and it inventories not so much ships as the names of the commanders from different areas of Greece and the nameless soldiers led by them,whose numbers are suggested by the number of vessels."

— Zholkovsky, Alexander. (2018). Listomania, Or Catalog as Technique (With Examples from Poetry and Prose, Classical and Modern, Western and Russian). Russian Journal of Linguistics. 22. 945-965. 10.22363/2312-9182-2018-22-4-945-965.

What are some ideas of archives that you can create?

  • website of archived writing entries
  • photography series of your collection of artifacts you have crafted yourself
  • drawings that are inspired by a collection of diary entries made into gifs
  • a monthly newsletter to your friends of your weekly grocery lists

What form does the process of archiving your work take shape as?

  • categoizing your art or design work by subject, material, and date
  • writing descriptions including titles, statements, and documentation of the process
  • determinining the nomenclature and organization of information (ex. the way libraries use a single, destinct spelling of a name or heading or a numeric identifier for each topic) ie. authority control
